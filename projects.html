Rov was a project created for DementiaHacks 2015. Our research showed that dementia patients tend to wander around the hospital and create issues for nurses and hospital staff. The Rov was intended to be a small, lightweight tracking device to be implemented into medical bracelets in order for staff to quickly and easily note the locations of patients and other staff. It utilized LightBlueBeans as iBeacons and a Raspberry Pi as a centralized server in order to dynamically track position using a triangulation formula based off of the signal strength.

ThermoVest

The ThermoVest was my first year design project in Biomedical Engineering. We were tasked with creating a device to help a paralympian. My research yielded that Paralympic athletes with spinal cord injuries have difficulty regulating temperature. To combat this, my team created an automated thermoregulatory harness complete with temperature sensors to monitor temperature and heating and cooling mechanisms which are automatically triggered when the body temperature is detected to be outside of an idea homeostatic range.

When my uncles first came into my parents' new house with drills, arduinos and wires aplenty I was confused and skeptical of their project. But they explained to me how they wanted to wire up our entire house to be controlled on a webserver. I wanted to help and when they had finished, they had it working on the webserver but had no interface for it, and it had to be controlled using complex commands into the address bar. I knew that there was no way my parents, or anyone else who came in our house, would be able to use it. I offered to make an interface for it, and considering I had just learned how to make AJAX requests, it was perfect timing! The interface has buttons which send the command to the webserver and read the response to update the UI.
